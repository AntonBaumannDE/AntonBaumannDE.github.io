Professional summary

I am a research engineer at heart and enjoy automation through information- and human-driven priors. At the moment I am working at Mech-Mind Robotics, where I build custom vision and robotics applications where I usually build and train different ML models at a fast-pace. I enjoy working in cross-disciplinary and innovative teams in order to solve complex, technological problems in a collaborative manner.

Before that I researched at Siemens where I implemented various, novel ML algorithms for things like meta-learning. In 2019, I successfully finished my apprenticeship as electrician for automation systems. In 2018, I interned on a record-breaking construction of a powerplant in Alexandria, Egypt. From 2016 to 2019, I also volunteered as a weightlifting coach at Fitlounge.

Research and academic interests

My primary research interest evolves around the challenge of building intelligent systems, that can interact with the real world and assist people in their daily lives. In particular, I am interested in designing sample-efficient and scalable learning algorithms using multimodal sensory data, previous learnings, demonstrations and vision.

I am currently looking into synthetic scene generation for simulation environments and creating novel datasets for cognitive systems.
