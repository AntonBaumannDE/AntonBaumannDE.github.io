Professional summary

At the moment I am working at Siemens as an undergraduate research engineer. I implement machine learning algorithms on robots for perception and control. I enjoy working in cross-disciplinary and innovative teams in order to solve complex, technological problems in a collaborative manner.

In 2019, I successfully finished my apprenticeship as electrician for automation systems. In 2018, I interned on one of the worlds biggest powerplants in Alexandria, Egypt. From 2016 to 2019, I also volunteered as a weightlifting coach at Fitlounge.

Research and academic interests

My primary research interest evolves around the challenge of building intelligent robots, that can interact with the real world and assist people in their daily lives. In particular, I am interested in designing sample-efficient and scalable learning algorithms using multimodal sensory data, previous learnings and vision. I am experienced in solving contact-rich manipulation tasks in unstructured environments.

I am looking for research opportunities in an academic or corporate institution, starting in July 2020.
